Where Diplomacy Meets History

We believe learning history shouldn't just be about memorizing dates. Our summer club combines rigorous historical research with the thrilling mechanics of Model United Nations, teaching students to collaborate, negotiate, and lead.

The Summer Intensive Syllabus

Five days. Five historic crises. One unmissable simulation experience. Check out our comprehensive week-long track below.

Monday

Boston Tea Party & American Revolution

Step into the high-stakes debate between colonial patriots and the British Crown. Navigate taxation without representation, trade embargoes, and the spark of revolution.

Focus: Diplomacy & Trade
Tuesday

The Congress of Vienna

Reconstruct the shattered map of Europe following the Napoleonic Wars. Balance competing imperial ambitions to forge a lasting continental peace.

Focus: European Geopolitics
Wednesday

The Great Depression

Simulate the emergency summits of the 1930s. Protect international markets, battle skyrocketing unemployment, and negotiate complex economic rescue packages.

Focus: Global Macroeconomics
Thursday

The 2008 Financial Crisis

Take control of central banks and global ministries during the modern era's most volatile market crash. Prevent a total systemic collapse through rapid diplomacy.

Focus: Trade & Monetary Systems
Friday

Pax Americana

Navigate the modern architecture of global governance, international treaties, and collective security alliances under the contemporary world system.

Focus: Global Security & Diplomacy
